CoronaVirus [ COVID-19 ]

. COVID-19 is a respiratory illness caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. Symptoms can range from mild (or no symptoms) to severe illness. Some common symptoms include fever, cough, and shortness of breath. Other symptoms can include fatigue, body aches, headache, loss of taste or smell, sore throat, congestion or runny nose, nausea or vomiting, and diarrhea.

 

 

. COVID-19 virus spreads primarily through droplets of saliva or discharge from the nose when an infected person coughs or sneezes. It’s also possible to get COVID-19 by touching a surface or object that has the virus on it and then touching your mouth, nose, or eyes.

 

 

. COVID-19 also spreads when an infected person breathes out droplets and very small particles that contain the virus. These droplets and particles can be breathed in by other people or land on their eyes, noses, or mouth. In some circumstances, they may contaminate surfaces they touch so be aware of whatever you touch

 

 

 

. If you think you have COVID-19 or have been exposed to someone with COVID-19, you should get tested and stay home and away from others until you receive your test results. If you test positive for COVID-19, you should isolate yourself to prevent the spread of the virus. If you develop severe symptoms such as difficulty breathing or persistent chest pain, seek medical attention immediately.
